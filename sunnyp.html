Computer Graphics

sub-field of computer science about about synthesizing and manipulating visual content
applied to provide visual information to users on computational environment

Two-Dimensional(2D)

there are 2 ways of showing digital images on computer: raster graphics and vector graphics



raster graphics

A set of digital values(also called picture elements or dots or pixels), containing fixed number of rows and colums of pixels.
Majority of images shown on computer screen are raster images
Raster images are created by input devices such as digital cameras, scanners.
Also synthesized from arbitrary non-image data such as mathmatical functions, geometric models.
Example of raster graphics file format: jpeg, png, apng, gif, mpeg4

vector graphics

Based on mathematical geometry(vector) to create images. Has points ,lines and curves to form the shapes.
Each of the points are the positions on x-axis and y-axis of the work plane, defining the direction and the path.
Each of the path also may have values of stroke color, shape, curve, thickness and fill.
Example of vector graphics file format: svg, eps, pdf

vector graphics and raster graphics are often combined in one image
